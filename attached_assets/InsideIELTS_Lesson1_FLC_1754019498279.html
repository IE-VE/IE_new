Lesson #1 – What is Fluency & Coherence (FLC)?

“What exactly is the examiner listening for?”

Each of the four IELTS speaking assessment criteria is broken down into language features.

In the speaking test the examiner is listening to your responses to see if you display the abilities described by the features at each level.

Fluency is about the ‘flow’ of your speech. The examiner is listening for two things:
  1. How well you are able to keep going – keep talking without running out of things to say.
  2. How often you hesitate (pause), repeat yourself, and self-correct your language when you talk.
Coherence is about how well you ‘stick your speech together’ using cohesive devices. The examiner is listening for:
  • Use of a range of spoken discourse markers and cohesive features.

There are many types of cohesive devices, including:

  • Conjunctions: e.g. and, but, so, if, because, additionally
  • Prepositions of sequence: e.g. to start with, secondly, next, after that
  • Referencing: using pronouns to refer back (e.g. he, she, it, that)

Examples:

  • “I practice speaking with my friend, she helps me every day.”
  • “My IELTS test is next month and I am confident I will pass it.”

Making flexible use of simple words like these is a key feature of a Band 6 candidate.
